What is QTEM?

Despite being a mouthful of buzzwords (Quantitative Techniques for Economics and Management) that make a nice sounding acronym, the QTEM Network Masters network is not a standalone degree or a secret society for quants. At its core, it’s a structured program that sits on top of an existing master’s program, stitching together quantitative training, international study, and applied experience across a set of partner institutions.

It certainly influenced my decision to pursue a M.Sc. in Financial Engineering at HEC Montréal, as it offered the option (not the obligation) to plug into something broader if I chose to do so.

The QTEM network is made up of a few main components:

  • A home master’s degree (in my case, Financial Engineering)
  • A mandatory exchange semester at one of the partner universities, with a strong emphasis on quantitative coursework
  • A quantitative internship, intended to ground the theory
  • A data-driven group challenge (GBAC), framed around a real company problem

In my eyes, QTEM is essentially an attempt to formalize something many students try to assemble informally: a network that provides technical depth, international connections, and applied work within a coherent structure.

At its best, it connects students, faculty, and corporate partners across countries without being anchored to any one institution, an ambition that’s earned it recognition from the Financial Times.
